Tails From Doha

Hugo

Hugo was found, newborn, still bloody and with his umbilical cord still attached, on a concrete road within the compound, pressed up against the curb! His sister Duma was found in a similar state, only she was being carried around and played with by some young local children. Kind local teens helped me gather up the two kittens and search the compound for any more that might be lying around - but we didn't find any. Nor did we find any trace of the mother cat - we had no idea who she might be.

Nervously, I cleaned up the kittens and my family and I bought some kitten formula for them. I was in quite a worried state over whether I had what it takes to successfully keep two newborns alive with no prior experience, when Cheshire walked into our home looking a bit dishevelled. On a hunch, I presented her with the two kittens, and she immediately began nursing them. We'd found Mum!

Hugo and Duma stayed with us up until we left Doha, growing into healthy young cats and getting all their medical needs seen to. They were then passed onto QAWS - the Qatar Animal Welfare Society - who took over our campaign to find them a forever home together.
