Montessa

When I first met Montessa, she was terrified of people, did not understand the concept of playing with cat toys, always cowered behind her mother Cheshire when I fed the street cats, and couldn't be indoors with the doors closed without absolutely freaking out. She was the first cat in Doha that I dedicated my time to taming, and I was so happy when I gained her trust enough that she would let me put a collar on her (to stop pest control taking her), let me take her to the vet for vaccinations, microchipping and eventual desexing, and would even sleep in my room and let my family and I pet her (for short amounts of time).

Montessa, just like her own mum, was a wonderful mother to both her own kittens - Tic Tac and Monkey - and to her sister Bandit's kittens - Freckles, Lotte and Teddy, who sadly had to be removed from Bandit to stop them being drowned by security. Tragically, a third kitten belonging to Montessa, Dottie, passed away just a few days after birth.

While I wasn't able to find Montessa a permanent home before I had to leave Doha, several local families took over her care.
